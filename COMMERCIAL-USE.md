## You Are Free to Use OpenLithoHub Commercially

OpenLithoHub is available under permissive open-source licenses:

- **Code**: Apache License 2.0
- **Documentation**: Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0
- **Datasets**: each dataset retains its original license — see
  [DATA-LICENSES.md](DATA-LICENSES.md). OpenLithoHub does not redistribute
  datasets; it provides adapters that download from each dataset's official
  source.

**No additional commercial license is required to use the open-source
version.** You may freely use, modify, and integrate OpenLithoHub into
commercial products, internal tooling, or research workflows — including
inside proprietary EDA, OPC, ILT, or mask-tapeout pipelines — with attribution
only.

## What Is Required

Attribution — include a notice in your product (e.g., "About" page,
documentation, or release notes) that references this project:

> This product includes OpenLithoHub
> (https://github.com/OpenLithoHub/OpenLithoHub), licensed under
> Apache License 2.0.

The [`NOTICE`](NOTICE) file must be included with any distribution of the
software per Apache 2.0 Section 4(d).

## Sustainability Model

OpenLithoHub uses a **CLA + Dual Licensing** model (similar to Qt, GitLab,
and other successful open-source projects):

- The **open-source version** remains 100% free under Apache 2.0
  (CC-BY-SA 4.0 for documentation) — forever. This will never change.
- **Commercial licensing revenue** funds continued development, benchmark
  curation, and community support.
- Contributors sign a [CLA](CLA-INDIVIDUAL.md) granting the maintainers the
  right to offer dual licenses. Contributors retain full ownership of their
  work, and their code always remains available under the open-source
  license.

We believe this model keeps the project sustainable long-term while ensuring
that the open-source community always has full access.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I integrate OpenLithoHub into a commercial EDA / OPC product?**
A: Yes. Apache 2.0 explicitly permits this. Attribution and the NOTICE file
are the only requirements for the open-source version.

**Q: Do I need to open-source my modifications?**
A: No. Apache 2.0 is permissive — you may keep modifications proprietary.
You must retain copyright notices and the NOTICE file.

**Q: Can my fab / mask-shop run OpenLithoHub on production tapeout flows?**
A: Yes, under the open-source license. For SLA-backed support and patent
indemnification, contact us about a commercial license.

**Q: When would I need a commercial license?**
A: Only if you want to remove the attribution requirement, need patent
indemnification, require SLA-backed support, or need access to proprietary
components. The open-source version is fully functional without a commercial
license.

**Q: Does the CLA mean my contributions become proprietary?**
A: No. Your contributions are always available under the open-source license
(Apache 2.0). The CLA additionally allows the maintainers to include your
code in the commercial offering. You retain full ownership of your work.

**Q: Can I redistribute datasets bundled with OpenLithoHub?**
A: OpenLithoHub does not bundle datasets — it provides adapters that
download from each dataset's official source. Each dataset retains its
original license. See [DATA-LICENSES.md](DATA-LICENSES.md).

**Q: Are pretrained model weights included?**
A: The open-source repository contains code and benchmarking infrastructure.
Some pretrained weights may be released under open licenses; others may be
available only under a commercial license — see model-specific
documentation.

**Q: Can I use OpenLithoHub for academic publications without a commercial
license?**
A: Yes. Academic use is fully covered by Apache 2.0. We ask that you cite
OpenLithoHub using [CITATION.cff](CITATION.cff).
